Nasi Goreng

Nasi goreng is a classic Indonesian fried rice dish made
with day-old rice, sweet soy sauce, eggs, and simple
aromatics like onion and garlic. Quick to prepare in one pan,
it’s a flavorful and comforting meal that can be
customized with chicken, shrimp, or vegetables,
and traditionally topped with a fried egg and crunchy prawn crackers.

Ingredients (2 people)

  • 2 bowls cooked rice (best if cold)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 onion (small, chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt & pepper
  • Optional: chicken, shrimp, or vegetables
\

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a big pan or wok.
  2. Scramble the eggs, take out, set aside.
  3. Fry onion and garlic until soft.
  4. Add rice, stir well so it doesn’t clump.
  5. Add kecap manis, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  6. Put the eggs (and any chicken/shrimp/veggies) back in. Stir-fry 2 minutes.
  7. Serve with a fried egg on top if you like.
